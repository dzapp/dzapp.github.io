A team of occupational and physical therapists from Alvernia University and Bellarmine University will be traveling to Belize to provide services and deliver rehabilitation care to the most vulnerable populations, including children and the poor.

About Belize

Belize, formerly British Honduras, is bordered by Mexico, the Caribbean Sea and Guatemala, and is the only English-speaking country in Latin America. Belize acquired its independence from Great Britain in 1981, and since that time Belizeans have struggled to rebuild and modernize their country’s sociopolitical and medical infrastructures.

Belize suffers from limited health care resources, and with more than half the population living in poverty, the resources are simply not there for quality medical care. The majority of the medical care can only be accessed in the capital, Belize City, and is provided by public hospitals that are plagued with equipment problems and supply shortages, including rehabilitation professionals.

People in need of rehabilitation services must generally travel elsewhere (e.g., the U.S. or Guatemala) for such care, primarily because there are few practicing physical therapists who live and work in the country on a full-time basis (5 or less licensed Physical therapists). Moreover, there are no academic programs to train physical or occupational therapists. Typically, rehabilitation field officers, who are home care providers employed by the public health care system and most often have little more than a high school education, provide rehabilitation.

About Our Community Partners

    Mercy Care Center 

  • A collaboration of Mercy Kitchen, established 1986, and Mercy Clinic, established 1959 is a Religious Order of the Sisters of Mercy sponsored center. The center is a non-profit organization and relies heavily on benefactors
  • The center responds to the needs of Belize City’s poor elderly. The kitchen provides for the nutritional needs and the clinic provides for the health needs of the patrons.
  • Mercy Clinic provides medical attention to registered patients and the programs offered to the elderly poor include out-patient services, medical care to homebound members and counseling.
  • Over the last four years, patients have received physical therapy services once a year through ISLE from Bellarmine DPT Program. AU DPT program is honored and excited to provide additional PT services and education in collaboration with Bellarmine DPT program beginning in January 2020.

    Inspiration Center

  • The Inspiration Center is the only organization to offer low-cost, comprehensive therapy and medical services to children with disabilities (birth to 16 years old) in the country of Belize.
  • t was founded in March 2014 by Mrs. Kim Simplis-Barrow, Belize’s Special Envoy for Women and Children and Global Ambassador for Special Olympics International, in collaboration with CARE-Belize, the Government of Belize and numerous donors and stakeholders.
  • The Inspiration Center serves children and families that have little access to therapy services and never denies care regardless of financial background.
  • Families and children travel for hours and sometimes days, after waiting over a year, for much needed servies and education.

    YWCA

  • The Belize YWCA was founded in 1956 and through the member’s commitment by faith, provide opportunities for the mental, physical, social and spiritual development of all, with a special emphasis on the empowerment of women and youth.
  • The Belize YWCA has programs that serve 2000 people directly – from infants to seniors. Some of these programmes are: Child Development, Community Development, Sustainable Skills Training, and Educational Classes.
  • The Belize YWCA has requested that we provide sustainable programing focusing on the following:
  • Education on allied health careers and opportunities, to provide the knowledge and skills necessary to achieve social and economic stability and development for women and girls;
  • Health, to promote wellness among women and girls psychologically, physically, and spiritually;
  • Institution/Organization: to build the capacity of the institution to provide quality service through educating caregivers on developmental screening, safe mobility training, and physical activity training.

    L.I.F.E. Living Independently in Full Existence

  • Improving the quality of life of seniors, (age 60 and older) living in the Southside of Belize City.
  • Through the collaborative efforts of community partners, LIFE will focus on measurably improving five dimensions that impact the health and well-being of older persons: 1) social supports; 2) housing; 3) food access; 4) medical care; and 5) transportation
  • We will be provided must needed PT/OT services to homebound seniors and education for the community workers on a variety of skills related to health and wellness of the seniors.
