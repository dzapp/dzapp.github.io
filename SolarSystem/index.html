Great Red Spot

The Great Red Spot is the most iconic place on Jupiter. It is wide enough it fit 2 earth's with winds up to about 400 mph this is not just a spot. The Great Red Spot has been spinning for about 150 as far as we know. Right now the Great Red Spot is huge mystery mostly becuase of Jupiters atmosphere, the fact that there is no ground, and that are telescopes can only see clouds high in the atmosphere.

Moons

Jupiter has the most moons of any planet in our solar system by a lot. There are 79 moons in total with 53 of them being named. Some of the most famous ones are Io, notable for its volcanos, Europa, with its icy surface, and many more notable moons like Gaymed and Callisto.

Light Shows

An incredible sight to see are at both poles of the massive planet. From Jupiter's huge magnetic field that is 20,000 times the strength of Earth's and many other factors these incredible auroras appear in both the north and south pole. They are caused by Jupiter's ability to produce it's own auroras and one of its moons Io that releases sulfur and oxygen at a huge rate. All of these factors lead to an icredible tourist attraction.

On its shell there is a protective exoskeleton that can withstands the extreme heat and cold of the planet. It breaths the hydrogen in the air through its gill on both sides. It is a very strong animal being able to live in the extreme winds of the inner planet but it raised as a child one of many particular moons. It thrives off of almost anything that lives in Jupiter's orbit. When they are little they eat the organisms that live on some of the moon and when they are adults they mostly eat/drink the ammonia ice found on the tops of the clouds.
