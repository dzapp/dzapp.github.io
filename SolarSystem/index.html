About Jupiter

The largest planet in the Milkey Way
Untouched. Unexplored.

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Milky Way by far. It can fit every planet in the solar system with ease. 1,300 earths could fit in Jupiter just for size. It is 498,710,653 miles from the sun lying 5th from the sun and the first of the gas giants. Taking about 640 days (over 1.5 years) to get to Jupiter from earth it is quite a long trip spanning 365,366 miles. Once you get there you will find the 4 layers as you get farther in; the exosphere, Thermosphere, Stratosphere, and Troposphere in the center. Each layer is categorized by pressure and temperature. Ammonia clouds causing the lightning are formed in the stratosphere and thermosphere. In the center of the planet is unknown to us but you can decide for yourself what you think it is. The exosphere is a frigid -234 degrees Fahrenheit and the core gets up to 43,000 degrees Fahrenheit . A day last only 10 hours but a year last 12 earth years.
