Aleph Zadik Aleph Five Folds of Programming (Check all that apply)

Social - Any programs where you socialize. (Just about all programs)

Athletic - Any sports or sports related event.

Education - Any program that teaches you something.

Judiac - Any program dealing with Judaism, Jewish culture, or Israel.

B'nai B'rith Girls Six Folds of Programming(Check all that apply)

Community Service - This fold can include anything from indirect service by raising money for charities to direct service. Community service programs can include raising money for charity, volunteering at a soup kitchen or seniors home. On going community service is recommended through TASC -Teens Actively Serving Communities.

Social Action - This fold enables BBGs to educate our members, voice our concerns and take action. Social action programming can include anything from doing an Aids awareness program, to creating petitions to send to political leaders, to having a speaker on date rape.

Recreation - Recreation programs are always very popular, they include social and athletic activities and can combine multiple folds. "Mind Body Attitude" which focuses on physical and mental health fitness of BBGs falls into the recreation fold.

Creativity - This fold creates the opportunity for members to discover and maximise their abilities and talents through imaginative programs that encourage members to express themselves in new ways. Creative programs can include fashion shows, drama games, mural making and more.

Jewish Heritage - This fold covers the totality of Jewish life. Programs range from holding your own service, to building a Chapter Sukkah, to educating members on Jewish concerns, to holding an "Israel Experience" night, to preparing a Chapter Shabbat dinner.

Sisterhood - This fold is the essence of B’nai B’rith Girls. It provides us with the opportunity to deal with issues that affect their development into Jewish women. This fold addresses issues such as women’s identity, interpersonal relationships, social roles, health issues, and our role in Judaism.
