About WIJABA

WIJABA is a charitable organization founded by one of my father's best friends, James Owens. James is an amazing person. He decided he wanted to promote literacy in places where books and reading are luxuries. He started building libraries in Indonesia seven years ago. Today WIJABA has opened 87 libraries, 2 mobile libraries, and 5 educational programs in 3 countries. As a result of his efforts, tens of thousands of young children have been exposed to the world of reading, which will open up doors for them in the future.

WIJABA to Me

I love to read and do research online; going online to websites and looking at the latest and greatest technologies in the world. I know how important and how much fun it is to read and do research, and I want other kids to have the same opportunities I have. This summer on my trip I met James and his son Alexander and found out about his charity WIJABA, which supports exactly this cause. Now that I know James and Alexander, supporting WIJABA is even more important to me.

My Project

For my Bar Mitvah project I've decided to raise money and awarness to help WIJABA. The money I raise will go towards building a new library in an elementary school in Chihuahua, Mexico, which is intended to open on Memorial Day 2017. The library is going to cost about $15,000 and my goal is to raise about $5,000 a third of the cost. For every donation made we will send you a bookmark to remind you how important it is to read, and that you are giving a child in Mexico a special opportunity.
